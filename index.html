Stage 1: The Excited Caleb

Often found at the beginning of every briefing, the Excited Caleb is
known to have way too many ideas for his upcoming projects. While in this
stage the Caleb is often distracted and is known to lose focus in classes,
often forgetting to pay attention to anything that the professor is saying.

Stage 2: The Finicky Caleb

Known by many as “the OCD idiot that wastes too much time shifting pixels”,
the Finicky Caleb can be found glued to his MacBook fine-tuning unnecessary details.
At the end of most projects, 75% of the work done by the Finicky Caleb never sees the
light of day. No one, not even the Caleb himself, really knows why this stage happens, but
the Finicky Caleb continues to insist that this is an important step in the creative process.

Stage 3: The Procrastinating Caleb

Having been lulled into a false sense of accomplishment by the Finicky Caleb,
the Procrastinating Caleb is hatched. The Procrastinating Caleb is known to have
terrible priorities and time management, and is often found binge-watching TV shows
and trash-talking people on the basketball court.

Stage 4: The Panicked Caleb

As the realization of imminent deadlines sets in, the Panicked Caleb arises from the ashes.
nown to sporadically drop F-bombs, the Panicked Caleb consumes at least two grande cups of
caffeine every day. During this stage, the Panicked Caleb somehow finds a way to become
nocturnal, often working through the night.

Stage 5: The Half-Awake Caleb

Often sighted on presentation days, the Half-Awake Caleb appears after a long night of
project work. Though having not slept at all, the Half-Awake is often able to function at a
surprisingly efficient level. However, upon arriving home the Half-Awake becomes the
Not Awake Caleb in the span of five minutes.
